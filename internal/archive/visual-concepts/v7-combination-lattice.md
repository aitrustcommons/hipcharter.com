# V7: Combination Lattice (Hasse Diagram of Power Set)

**Date:** March 16, 2026
**Concept:** Four rows converging to one center, inspired by Nikhil's whiteboard sketch. Mathematical structure: all combinations of 4 elements (C(4,1) + C(4,2) + C(4,3) + C(4,4) = 4+6+4+1 = 15).

Row 1: 4 singles (colored cards with Human + AI contributions)
Row 2: 6 pairs (all equal size, color dots showing parent petals)
Row 3: 4 triples (warm tint, converging)
Row 4: 1 center (full partnership, connects to Intent Layer below)

**How we got here:**
- V5 (spanning boxes from Nikhil's first whiteboard): right idea but width implied importance
- V6 (diamond network from Recurate multi-model consensus): equal edges but T-P and F-I crossed center
- V6b (curved edges): better but still crowded labels
- Nikhil's second whiteboard: "4 vertical rectangles, then 6 boxes, then 3 (actually 4) triples, then 1 center"
- Recognized as a combination lattice / Hasse diagram of the power set

**What works:**
- Every combination explicitly shown. No ambiguity.
- All boxes in a row are equal size. No bias.
- Natural funnel shape leads into Intent Layer.
- Triples included (V1-V6 had only pairs).
- Color dots on pair/triple cards trace lineage to parent petals.
- Extensible: 5 petals = 31 positions (5+10+10+5+1).
- Row counts (4, 6, 4, 1) are Pascal's triangle row 4.

**What needs work:**
- Connection lines between rows are complex. Simplified in poster version.
- Professional polish needed. V7 poster HTML is the starting point for Canva/design tool.

**Multi-model validation:** Threw the visualization problem at app.recurate.ai (Recurate Platform). Claude recommended force-balanced network graph. GPT recommended Venn. Grok recommended UpSet plot. Both Claude and Grok rejected 4-circle Venn for asymmetry. The lattice approach synthesizes the best of all suggestions: explicit combinations (like UpSet), equal treatment (like the network graph), and visual overlap story (like Venn) without the asymmetry.
